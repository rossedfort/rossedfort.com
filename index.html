I was born in Somerset, New Jersey in 1993. As a child, I had many interests, including art, nature, music, science, and athletics. The sports I tried varied, including Soccer, Tennis, Golf, and Swimming. However my true passion was Diving. From the age of 8, I was a Junior Olympic level diver, growing to be competitive at a national level. The highlight of my young athletic career was winning the Junior Olympic National Championship in 2006, when I was 13 years old. Around this time, some my other extra-curricular interests started to move to the back burner. I focused mainly on school and diving

In 2011, I decided to accept a scholarship to the University of Denver as a Division-I Diver, and full-time student. I studied Emergent Digital Practices, a blend of art, design, media, culture, and technology. In this program I learned to use software such as Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, and Audition to create compelling pieces of digital art. I leanred how to collaborate with my peers on long term projects, and I learned how to be effective in a Digital Age. My 4 years of Diving at a collegiate level taught me lessons in discipline, perserverence, and teammwork. As a captain of the team for my final 2 years of college, I learned how to lead a group of 25 swimmers and divers to success at a high level. Collegiate athletics taught me many valuable lessons, which are reflected in my professional career today.

Upon graduation in 2015, I was accepted to the Turing School of Software and Design, in Denver, Colorado. Turing is a 7-month developer training program, unlike any Dev Bootcamp in existence. Turing was a grueling but immensely rewarding academic endeavor, that not only taught me the technical skills necessary to become a developer, but the soft skills needed to thrive in any career. After finishing the program in March of 2016, I accepted a position at Wazee Digital, where I am a front-end software engineer today.
